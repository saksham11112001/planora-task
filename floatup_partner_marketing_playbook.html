⚡ The #1 Rule

For the first 30 days, never mention Floatup directly in posts. Build your audience as a CA who has strong opinions about practice management. The product mention comes naturally after trust is built. People buy from people, not from posts that read like ads.

🏛️ The 5 Content Pillars
1
Pain Point (40% of posts)
The struggles CAs face daily. GST deadlines, client document chasing, team coordination. You know these better than any marketer. Write like you're venting to a fellow CA. No solution needed — just the honest problem.
2
Insight / Opinion (25% of posts)
Strong takes on how CA practices should be run. "Most firms treat task management as an afterthought. Here's why that's killing their growth." Contrarian views generate comments. Comments generate reach.
3
Story (20% of posts)
Real stories — a client crisis, a deadline you nearly missed, the moment you decided to build Floatup. Stories get saved and shared more than any other content type. First-person, specific details, real emotion.
4
Community Question (10% of posts)
"What's your biggest challenge during ITR season?" Questions get 5x more comments than statements. Comments push your post to your connections' feeds. Pure reach multiplier.
5
Floatup Mention (5% of posts)
After 4-6 weeks of the above, your audience trusts you. NOW you can say "We built a tool for exactly this — floatup.app". It doesn't read like an ad because they know you. Keep these posts rare — 1 per month max initially.
✍️ LinkedIn Post Format That Works
Hook (Line 1 — most important)

Must work standalone. LinkedIn shows only the first 2 lines before "...see more". If the hook doesn't create curiosity or make them feel something, they won't click. Start with a number, a bold claim, or a relatable situation. Never start with "I".

❌ Bad hooks:

"I wanted to share something about my experience as a CA..."
"Today I'm going to talk about task management..."

✅ Good hooks:

"My client called me at 11:47 PM. GST was due at midnight."
"3 years as a CA. The hardest thing was never the accounting."
"Most CA firms will fail in the next 5 years. Here's why."

📱 YouTube Shorts & Instagram Reels Strategy

Shorts and Reels are your discovery engine. Long-form builds trust. Shorts/Reels bring new people in. You need both.

📐 Format Specs

YouTube Shorts: 9:16 vertical, 1080×1920px, under 60 sec, add #Shorts in title

Instagram Reels: 9:16 vertical, 1080×1920px, under 90 sec, captions on-screen

LinkedIn Video: landscape 16:9 or square 1:1, under 3 min works best

🎬 What makes a Short/Reel perform

• Hook in the first 2 seconds — text on screen or action
• No slow intros — start mid-action
• Captions always on (80% watch muted)
• End with a CTA: "Full tutorial in description"
• Keep energy up — cut dead air ruthlessly

Best Short/Reel formats for Floatup:
Format 1 — "Watch Me Do X in Under 30 Seconds"
Screen recording clip of a fast, satisfying action. Example: "Creating a full compliance task schedule for 10 clients in 28 seconds." No voiceover needed — just the screen recording with upbeat background music and captions. These perform extremely well because they're visual proof.
Format 2 — "Before vs After" (no speaking required)
Split screen or sequential clips. Left: WhatsApp chaos, missed deadline on Excel. Right: Floatup compliance dashboard, everything tracked. Text overlays tell the story. 30–45 seconds. Pure visual storytelling — highest share rate of any format.
Format 3 — Feature Spotlight (extracted from tutorial chapters)
Take the best 45–60 seconds from each tutorial chapter. Add AI voiceover from the narration script (same ElevenLabs audio, just the relevant section). Add a text hook at the top: "Did you know Floatup does this?" or "CAs are using this to eliminate deadline stress." Upload as Short + Reel.
Format 4 — "POV" format (trending, high reach)
Text on black screen or over video: "POV: You're a CA and it's 3 days before the GST deadline." Then show the Floatup dashboard — everything tracked, nothing missed, team on top of it. The contrast between the "POV" tension and the calm organised dashboard is the punchline. 20–30 seconds.
Format 5 — "3 Things" quick tip (for CA community building)
Does NOT need to mention Floatup. Example: "3 things CA firms do that kill their growth" or "3 signs your practice management system is broken." Pure value. Builds audience. End card: "We're building a solution — floatup.app." 45–60 seconds with AI voiceover from ElevenLabs.

📈 YouTube Growth — First 90 Days Reality Check
Honest expectations

New YouTube channels grow slowly. Expect 50–200 views per video in the first month. That's normal and fine. You're not building a YouTube influencer channel — you're building a search-discoverable product library. A CPA in Toronto who searches "CA compliance tracking software" and finds your Chapter 7 video has high purchase intent. 200 views of the right audience is worth more than 20,000 random views.

Month 1 — Plant the seeds
Upload Weeks 1–4 videos. Share each video on LinkedIn and in 2 CA communities. Goal: 200–500 total channel views. Don't check daily. Focus on quality uploads.
Month 2 — Algorithm starts noticing
If watch time is decent (people watching 60%+ of each video), YouTube starts suggesting your videos to similar viewers. Compliance Module video (Ch 7) likely gets traction via search. Goal: 1,000+ total channel views.
Month 3 — Compounding begins
Old videos start getting discovered via search. Shorts drive subscriptions. Playlist views increase. Goal: 100+ subscribers, consistent 50–100 views/video within 48 hours of upload. At this point your channel has real momentum.
